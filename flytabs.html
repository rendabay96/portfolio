FlyTabs - Mobile App

UX Design Concept Project

Traveling in The Social Media Era

People of all generations can attest that the world has never felt smaller than it does today. Not only are we inextricably linked through social media, our constant exposure to one another’s travel destinations inspire us to plan our next excursions with our favorite people. Having witnessed various ways people plan their individual as well as group travels, I saw the potential in designing a more streamlined and social process for organizing trips.

THE PROCESS

Understand the Users

There are several widely-used travel search engines that are already efficient in planning one’s individual travels. However, with so many options available to everyone, I wanted to better understand how they approached finalizing a travel itinerary, which would clarify what problems they face in the process. I sent a survey to 11 travel enthusiasts to learn the following:

  1. Find out how users search for flights and hotels.
  2. Compare ways that users save and share travel information with their group.
  3. Learn what users like about existing methods and what they wish could be improved.

Much of the results regarding how the users typically booked trips were as I suspected. 9 out of 11 people surveyed usually plan trips with 2 or more people. Of those people:

  • 70% indicated they prioritize finding the lowest cost flights, being on the same flight if possible, and sharing the group travel itinerary.
  • 100% share the flight and hotel options with each other, all of which indicated Facebook as their preferred method of sharing
  • 89% want the option to organize trips by name within a travel app
  • 78% want the option to share flight and lodging search results with group members via email or social media
  • 78% want the option to enable in-app price tracking
  • 67% want the option of flight searches to display flexible dates
