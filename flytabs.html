FlyTabs - Mobile App

UX Design Concept Project

People of all generations can attest that the world has never felt smaller than it does today. Not only are we inextricably linked through social media, our constant exposure to one another’s travel destinations inspire us to plan our next excursions with our favorite people. Having witnessed various ways people plan their individual as well as group travels, I saw the potential in designing a more streamlined and social process for organizing trips.

THE PROCESS

Understand the Users

There are several widely-used travel search engines that are already efficient in planning one’s individual travels. However, with so many options available to everyone, I wanted to better understand how they approached finalizing a travel itinerary, which would clarify what problems they face in the process. I sent a survey to 11 travel enthusiasts to learn the following:

  1. Find out how users search for flights and hotels.
  2. Compare ways that users save and share travel information with their group.
  3. Learn what users like about existing methods and what they wish could be improved.

Much of the results regarding how the users typically booked trips were as I suspected. 9 out of 11 people surveyed usually plan trips with 2 or more people. Of those people 100% of respondents selected Facebook as their current preferred method for planning a group trip. However, when asked what additional features they wished to see in a new group travel planning app, I received the following:

Proposed Feature Selected % of Respondents
Ability to share a group travel itinerary 70
Ability to book the same flights and/or hotels 70
Enable price tracking 78
Ability to share and discuss trip details with group members 78
Ability to organize trips by name 89
When asked how each of them saves their preferred flight or hotel search results, I received many different answers:

  • "...bookmark it"
  • "...e-mail the link to myself"
  • "...take a screenshot"
  • "...copy and paste the link to my notes app"
  • "...keep the tab open on my browser"
  • "...favorite the search result on the website"
  • "...post the link to the Facebook group"

Surely there must be a better way!

Recognize The Problem

According to the respondents, while planning a trip for oneself was easy enough, doing so for a group has always been a cumbersome process. Initially, my vision for FlyTabs was a new tool that maintained all functions of a typical travel app (search, save, and book flights and hotels) with the added convenience of message integration to help users share and interact with friends. However, I realized after speaking with potential users that the design challenge lied not with improving the booking process, but within the collaborative planning phase of a group trip.

Personas

In addition to analyzing the survey data, I interviewed my respondents so that they can elaborate on their specific needs. Along with the usual search engine attributes, users are eager for a search and book engine to offer the organizational and social aspects of FlyTabs to ease the group trip planning process. From here I continued to design a solution with these personas in mind.
