Branding + Landing Page Design

Moshu

It has become the norm for online shoppers to thoroughly research a product before purchasing, so ecommerce brands must use creative ways to win over their customers. For custom-made products, the key is transparency and involving the customer throughout the production process. With these challenges in mind I designed a landing page for my passion project: Moshu, a custom toy web app.

Getting Started

User Research - Online Shoppers

To design this product showcase landing page, I needed to research what users currently consider a great online shopping experience. Through outreach on Reddit and Facebook I collected 31 surveys from people ages 18 - 64 and learned the following:

  • 97% of users want to see a preview of the item

  • 75% want tracking info on progress & shipping

  • 71% value customer reviews and photos

The Objective

The product showcase landing page should quickly be able to create a lasting impression on its viewers and convert them to transacting customers. According to my user research, the product landing page of a custom product (i.e. Moshu's custom-made stuffed animals) should also create trust by offering an interactive shopping experience to allow the user a sense of ownership and ability to customize and track the order.

Branding

The Look

In order to create a whimsical look, I chose a bold headline plus a geometric typeface for the body text that I associated with drawing and creativity. As for the palette, light pastels and subtle primary colors were a modern version of classic toy-themed color schemes. Designing the Experience

User Flows and Site Map

As research for the product, I drafted personas and mapped out the user flows and site map which encompassed the full user journey starting from creating the account to designing, purchasing, and tracking the custom order.

Testing with Wireframes

Before mocking up the landing page, I wanted to better understand the customer's entire shopping journey. I created a low-fidelity prototype of Moshu for the first round of usability testing and asked users to complete the following tasks:

  • Browse and learn about the product

  • Create your custom plush toy

  • Purchase and track your order

Despite the low-fidelity nature of this prototype, nearly all users were able to navigate through the website and eventually complete their purchase.
