Moshu - Product Landing Page

UX Design Concept Project

It has become the norm for customers to thoroughly research the product they’ll eventually buy online. Ecommerce brands today must promote a higher degree of transparency to retain the trust of their audience in a world where online shopping decisions are influenced by customer reviews. For customizable products, an additional concern is simply involving the customer throughout the production process. I saw an opportunity to design a product showcase landing page that addresses all of these challenges in an increasingly competitive landscape for online brands.

The inspiration behind the product advertised in this independent project came from my own wishful thinking as a child and the inspiring doodles created by two adorable toddlers of a friend of mine. When propositioned with the possibility that one day, one of their drawings may come to life in the form of a 3D toy, kids and adults alike beamed with excitement. This product would encourage creative expression for kids in an age where toddlers learn to navigate apps faster than their millennial parents.

GETTING STARTED

Understand the Users

In my quest to design a comprehensive landing page experience, I also wanted to gauge interest in the product I had in mind.

  • What do users like/dislike about current customized online shopping experiences?
  • What aspects of a product landing page attract customers most?
  • What would users want in a webapp for designing custom gifts?
  • Are people interested in custom-made toys?

Through outreach on Reddit and Facebook I collected 31 surveys from people ages 18 - 64, and of these respondents 100% have purchased something online. While most data turned out as expected, I made some discoveries that (see below).

Three most popular online retailers:

  • 100% have shopped online - on Amazon (100%), eBay (74%), Etsy (32%)
  • 55% of the respondents have purchased or received a custom order (such as monogrammed gifts or custom clothing), and most have had positive experiences.

When I interviewed some users why they have not purchased custom orders online, answers, for the most part, involved either not coming across the opportunity to do so or preferring to do so in person i.e. try clothing personally.

To further explore what features users would like to see that may or may not already exist on their current prefered online shopping experiences, I also wanted to get a grasp of what might convert people to buying more custom items online.:
  • An overwhelming majority (97%) want the ability to see a preview of the item
  • 75% want tracking info on the progress and shipping of their item
  • 71% say their decision is affected by viewing customer reviews and photos
  • And surprisingly, the majority (54%) answered that they are indifferent toward stores that serve a charitable cause

The Goal

The product showcase landing page needs to gain the trust of customers and find clever ways through its storytelling, level of interaction, and transparency to drive purchases. Especially within the custom order realm (i.e. custom stuffed animals), the product landing page requires an interactive shopping experience to allow customizability and progress tracking.

THE RESEARCH

Know the Target Market

In addition to analyzing the survey data, I interviewed my respondents so that they can elaborate on their specific needs. Along with the usual search engine attributes, users are eager for a travel app to offer the organizational and social aspects of FlyTabs to ease the group trip planning process. These are people who embrace social media platforms (like Facebook) and their group activity functions but require much more when it comes to planning travel. From here I proceeded to design a solution with these personas in mind.

DESIGNING THE EXPERIENCE

User Flows and Site Map

With personas and user stories in mind, I mapped out the user flows for Moshu. In order to best understand the product being showcased, I wireframed the full experience from onboarding to creating a new toy to finalizing the transaction. All steps were accounted for and prepped to create my first low-fidelity wireframes for Moshu. REPLACE IMGS

Testing with Wireframes

Using Sketch, I designed low-fidelity wireframes for all user flows. I then created a clickable prototype using InvisionApp for my first round of usability testing. The purpose of testing early is to capture any areas of improvement before diving too deep into designing. I asked five testers to comment aloud as they went through several user flows prompted by a script I wrote for user testing.
